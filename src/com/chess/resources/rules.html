Following rules are implemented:

General rule sets

start of the game

  • player with white pieces starts.
  • the start situation will determine the move direction of pieces. (specifically pawns)

during the game

  • a piece can be hit if another piece is moved onto that specific field. (exception: en passant move)
  • if the player picks up a piece, he is forced to make a move with this piece. (this rule can be disabled)
  • CHECK: if a king is endangered by his opponent, he is forced to get out of that situation.

end of the game

  • CHECKMATE: the game ends with a winner, if - during the next move - the king of the other player would be killed.
  • STALEMATE: if no valid moves are possible and the king is not in check, the game ends with a draw.
  • REMIS: if too many meaningless moves (50 moves without a kill and no pawn moved or same position 3 times repeated) are made, the game will end with a draw. (this rule can be disabled)

Moves by piece

pawns

  • pawns can move one step forward only. in case it is their first move, two steps forward are allowed. (see red arrows)
  • pawns can hit other pieces diagonally (just one step allowed, see purple arrow).
  • EN PASSANT MOVE: if a pawn of the other player did a long starting move during the last turn and our pawn is next to it (left or right), we can kill the other pawn. see situation in blue square: if the black pawn is doing an en passant move, he would land on the field with the blue flash. (this move can be disabled on the settings screen)
  • PROMOTION: if a pawn reaches the end of the board, it will become a queen.

rooks

  • rooks can move/hit straight in any direction. multiple steps at once are allowed, if the passed way is free.
  • rooks are also involved in the castling move. (see below)

knights

  • knights can move/hit one step diagonally plus one step straight (away from the current position). both part-steps account as one step.
  • knights are actually the only pieces being able to leap over other pieces. (no matter which colour)

bishops

  • bishops can move/hit diagonally in any direction. multiple steps at once are allowed, if the passed way is free.

queens

  • queens can move/hit straight and diagonally in any direction. multiple steps at once are allowed, if the passed way is free.
  • as you see the queen's got game, take care over her.

kings

  • kings can move/hit straight and diagonally in any direction. just one step at once is allowed.
  • if a king is in check, the according player is forced to get out of the check situation.
  • CASTLING: a king can do a castling move with a rook, as long as both of the pieces were never moved before and the way between is free.
    a castling move can only be done if the king is not in check at the beginning or during the castling move.
    a castling move has to be triggered by moving the king.
